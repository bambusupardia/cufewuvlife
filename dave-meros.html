Dave Meros Biography

Dave Meros is one of the most popular and richest American bass player who was born on February 8, 1956 in Salinas, California, United States. Dave Meros (born 8 February 1956) Dave Meros (born 8 February 1956) was an American bass guitarist popular as the bassist of progressive rock group Spock’s Beard. Meros is currently the bass player of Iron Butterfly and has also recorded or played with artists like Gary Myrick, Bobby Kimball of Toto, Simon Phillips, Steve Lukather, Michael Landau, Glenn Hughes, Mark Lindsay of Paul Revere and the Raiders and Big Big Train, and played with Eric Burdon and The Animals for more than 16 years. He was also the tour manager for a number of these years and also served as an assistant tour manager for other artists too. As a bassist Meros is influenced by music diverse, including Paul McCartney, John Entwistle, Chris Squire, James Jamerson, Marcus Miller, Francis “Rocco” Prestia of Tower of Power, Chuck Rainey and David Hungate.

Meros is currently a member of the Sacramento area the band Rolling Heads featuring Spock’s Beard bandmate Ted Leonard and former Spock’s Beard member Jimmy Keegan. Meros is also part of Iron Butterfly, having previously played with them as a replacement for Late Lee Dorman during the band’s 2006 tour.

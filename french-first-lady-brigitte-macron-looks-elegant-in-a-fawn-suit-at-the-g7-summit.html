French First Lady Brigitte Macron looks elegant in a fawn suit at the G7 summit

The French First Lady looked a picture of elegance today in a fawn suit as she joined the UK Prime Minister’s wife and FLOTUS for a performance at the Minack Theatre while in Cornwall for the G7 summit.

Brigitte Macron, 68, teamed the beige two-piece with a white organza lace blouse and chic pearl espadrilles. 

It’s likely they’re from Castañer, a Spanish brand favoured by the Duchess of Cambridge, costing £110. Mrs Macron previously wore a white pair from the fashion house last year.

She wore her glossy blonde bob loose and sported a pair of large black round RayBan sunglasses to shield her eyes from the glare of the Cornish sun.

Former teacher Brigitte, who is married to French President Emmanuel Macron, kept her accessories to a minimum, wearing a sleek gold watch and her dazzling engagement and wedding rings.

It’s not known where Mrs Macron’s suit is from, but the French First Lady often wears custom Louis Vuitton.

Sat on tartan blankets, newlywed Carrie Johnson, Dr Jill Biden and Brigette were treated to a performance of Ocean World at the picturesque seaside venue, and chatted to performers afterwards.

The trio were socially distanced in the unique open air theatre carved into the granite cliff at Porthcurno, close to Land’s End, and appeared in high spirits as they took in the show.

Earlier in the week Brigitte joined her husband at a G7 reception at the Eden Project in Cornwall, following the start of the summit.

Again she kept her colour palette simple, donning a cream high neck top decorative gold buttons across the collarbone, with a complimenting skirt.

For the formal occasion, Brigitte swept her locks into a chic updo and carried a small clutch bag.

This afternoon Boris and Carrie Johnson sweltered in the Cornwall sunshine today as they welcomed more high-powered G7 guests ahead of a beach barbecue. 

Any jokes with Emmanuel Macron about sausages could be a dangerous move, as the UK and EU leaders are engaged in a bitter row over Brexit restrictions on chilled meat exports from the British mainland to Northern Ireland.

Earlier, Mr Johnson demanded the EU ‘gets it into their heads’ that Northern Ireland is part of the UK – as he branded the bloc’s approach to the Brexit rules ‘theological’ in a round of broadcast interviews after holding talks with Mr Macron, Angela Merkel and EU chief Ursula von der Leyen.

Tonight’s much-anticipated barbecue comes as the UK recorded a further 7,738 new Covid-19 cases – the fourth day in a row that cases have exceeded the 7,000 mark.
